Do While loop:

If you understood the while loop, you should have no trouble understanding the do-while loop, because they are almost exactly the same. The only difference is that the condition for the do-while loop comes at the end and not at the beginning like for the while loop. This guarantees at least one iteration will occur for do-while loops.




First notice the format of the do-while. It starts with the keyword “do” followed by the opening brace. The contents of the loop are, as always, written between the opening and closing brace. The condition, however, is after the closing brace. It should also be noted that a semi-colon is necessary after the condition, something commonly left out be new coders.

As demonstrated above, the do-while guarantees at least one iteration because the condition is checked only after the first iteration. Here ‘num’ start as 0, but is still printed, even though the condition asks for num to be greater than 0. The condition is not checked until after it’s printed once, and then of course it fails and the loop terminates.
